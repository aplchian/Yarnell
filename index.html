Sable Dunn likes to write anonymous letters, sneak into vacant houses, make prank phone calls, meditate on crystals, spy from rooftops.

But will her clandestine actions help to find her tribe? Will they bring her closer to her girlhood crush, Aaron? Or her mysterious neighbor, Skot? Or the rebel chick at school, Robin? Or perhaps Wynn and his cohorts from across the bridge of her quirky hometown have some lessons to give her with jumping to conclusions.

Just as Sable makes progress with her quest for belonging, she questions how love—in all its many forms—fits in with the pursuit of purpose and discovers the price of satiating her curiosity.

Biography

a short biography

Emerging from the Ashes of Deep Cuts and Buttered Popcorn

Alika Yarnell grew up listening to an obscene amount of “New Wave,” “Synth Pop,” and “Goth” cuts in her home town of Coronado, California, the city in which her fictionalized Glorietta is based. She received her MFA from the University of San Francisco and has published several short stories in various literary journals. Raindrop Parade is her first novel.

When she’s not writing or jamming to the latest tracks in the indie scene (“Psych Rock” and “Chillwave” being her favorite subgenres), she practices yoga (from arm balances to corpse pose), devours films (or the new art house movies: premium cable series), and adds to her rock collection (including several crystal recorders and Lemurian shards).

She currently lives in San Diego and is working on her next book, which includes some familiar characters in addition to a whole new cast of oddities.
